# Read Figure E7-1 by nodes

Circuit diagrams compress physical connections into symbols and junctions. Start by locating the named terminals, the supply rail, and every ground symbol. Then trace one junction at a time. Lines meeting at a filled dot share a node; a component symbol separates the nodes on its two sides.

In Figure E7-1, the input reaches the transistor through C1 and the output leaves through C2. R1 and R2 meet the transistor's left-side node. A separate vertical resistor joins the upper-right transistor node to the positive rail. R3 and C3 meet the lower-right transistor node and continue toward ground.

This node inventory is descriptive. It does not by itself assign a purpose to a resistor or name the amplifier configuration. Those conclusions require comparing where the signal enters and leaves, which lead is shared by the input and output paths, and how each resistor connects to the supply and ground.
